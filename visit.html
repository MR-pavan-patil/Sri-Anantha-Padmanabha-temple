How To Reach

Finding Your Way to
the Sacred Forest

Sri Anantha Padmanabha Temple is situated in a quiet forested sanctuary approximately 15 km from Bidar city center. The location is well connected by multiple transit networks.

🚗

By Road

Excellent road connectivity from Bidar city. Local taxis, auto-rickshaws, and private vehicles can travel directly up to the temple's forest parking lot via Bidar-Udgir road.

Distance: ~15 KM from Bidar
🚂

By Train

Bidar Railway Station (BDCR) is the closest station, connected to major railway lines in Karnataka and neighboring regions (Hyderabad, Bangalore, Mumbai, Pune).

Distance: ~16 KM from Station
🚌

By Bus

KSRTC and private bus networks operate regular services from Hyderabad, Gulbarga, and Bangalore to the main Bidar Bus Stand. Local town buses travel to nearby village stops daily.

Distance: ~15.5 KM from Bus Stand
✈️

By Air

Rajiv Gandhi International Airport in Hyderabad (HYD) is the nearest international airport. Bidar airport has limited flights but Hyderabad offers global taxi connections.

Distance: ~150 KM (3.5 hrs drive)
Visitor Information

Essential Pilgrimage Details

Familiarize yourself with temple customs, prasadam timings, dress rules, and available facilities to ensure a comfortable visit.

🪔 General Customs

  • Entry Fee: Entrance to the temple and trail is completely free of cost for all visitors.
  • Dress Code: Devotees are requested to dress modestly in traditional Indian attire. Avoid clothing that is short, transparent, or tight-fitting.
  • Photography: Permitted in the outer campus gardens and trails, but strictly prohibited inside the main sanctum sanctorum.
  • Footwear: Shoes and sandals must be deposited at the free footwear counter before stepping into the inner courtyard.

🪷 Annadanam Prasadam

Serving food to pilgrims is considered one of the highest acts of spiritual service. The temple trust offers Free Annadanam (hot sacred meals) to every visitor daily.

  • Daily Timings: 12:30 PM to 2:30 PM.
  • Location: The spacious Annadanam Dining Hall located on the right side of the main temple complex.
  • Special Days: Extended dining hours and special sweet items served during Ekadashis and major festival weeks.

Guidelines

Do's & Don'ts for Pilgrims

Help us maintain the spiritual holiness and natural purity of this sacred grove temple. Please adhere to these guidelines.

✓ Recommended (Do's)

  • Dress modestly in appropriate traditional Indian clothing.
  • Maintain strict silence and devotions while inside the sanctum area.
  • Use the waste bins situated along the pathway to throw trash.
  • Respect the local forest flora and fauna; walk quietly on the designated trails.
  • Cooperates with temple authorities and security guards on duty.

✗ Prohibited (Don'ts)

  • Do not throw plastic, bottles, or paper trash inside the sacred forest paths.
  • Do not feed the wild monkeys, peacocks, or birds; let them feed naturally.
  • Do not photograph or film the deities inside the inner sanctum.
  • Do not carry single-use plastic bags, matchboxes, or cigarettes into the trails.
  • Do not enter temple bounds under the influence of alcohol or toxic substances.
