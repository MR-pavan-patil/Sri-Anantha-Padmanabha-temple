Manik Prabhu Mandir

A sacred center of interfaith harmony, dedicated to the teachings and samadhi of Saint Shri Manik Prabhu.

Sacred Chronicle

History & Heritage

The Divine Incarnation of Shri Manik Prabhu

Shri Manik Prabhu Maharaj was born in 1817 in the Bidar district. From early childhood, he showed miraculous spiritual powers and spent a lot of time wandering in the hills and forests of Bidar (including the Velaspur-Mailar jungles) in a state of deep meditation (Samadhi). He is globally recognized as the 4th avatar of Lord Dattatreya in the glorious master-lineage.

Because of his divine radiance, people started calling him "Manik" (a precious gemstone). He established his main spiritual seat at Maniknagar (Humnabad), but this particular mandir/ashram near the Velaspur forest marks the sacred spot where the Guru stayed, meditated, and blessed the local rural and tribal communities during his spiritual travels.

Teachings

Philosophy of Royal Mysticism (Raja Yogi)

👑 The "Abadhoot" Style

Unlike typical ascetics who lived in extreme poverty, Shri Manik Prabhu lived like a king (Raja Yogi). He sat on a royal throne and wore rich clothes, yet he was completely detached from material wealth. He gave away everything he received to the poor and needy.

🤝 Ganga-Jamuni Tehzeeb (Communal Harmony)

Shri Manik Prabhu is highly respected by both Hindus and Muslims. Local Nizams, Nawabs, and Sufi saints used to visit him. In this ashram, you will find an environment where people of all faiths sit together on the same floor, breaking all barriers of caste and creed.

🎉 Festivals & Traditions

  • Shri Datta Jayanti (Manik Jayanti Mahotsav)
    Celebrated with extreme grandeur in December (Margashirsha). The entire temple is decorated, and a golden/silver palanquin (Pallaki Utsav) of the Guru is taken out, drawing lakhs of devotees from across the Deccan.
  • Guru Poornima
    A massive gathering takes place where devotees offer their deep respects to the holy sandals (Padukas) of Shri Manik Prabhu.
  • The Tradition of Annadan (Free Kitchen)
    Following the core philosophy of Manik Prabhu Maharaj—"No one should leave the Guru’s door hungry"—this temple serves free, high-quality, hot meals (Mahaprasad) daily to all visitors.
Travel Guide

How to Reach & Connectivity

📍

From Mailar / Gaimukh

It is located directly within the same travel route. After finishing darshan at Mailar Mallanna and taking a holy dip at Gaimukh, you can reach this peaceful mandir within 5-10 minutes via local transport.

🚌

By Road

Situated smoothly along the highway connectivity turning towards the Mailar-Velaspur forest area from Bidar city center (~16-18 km away).
