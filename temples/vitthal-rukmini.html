Vitthal Rukmini Mandir

A divine temple of love and devotion, echoing with soulful abhangas and devotional singing.

Sacred Chronicle

History & Heritage

The Historical Connection to Pandharpur

According to local lore, the history of this Vitthal Rukmini temple is deeply intertwined with the historic Bhakti Movement that swept across Maharashtra and Karnataka between the 13th and 17th centuries. Since Bidar shares its border closely with Maharashtra, the local pastoral and farming communities (who frequently travelled across borders) brought the worship of Lord Vitthala (Vithoba) here over a century ago.

A group of devoted pilgrims from the Mailar-Velaspur region who regularly walked the annual Vari (pilgrimage) to the famous Pandharpur temple were blessed by the Lord. To make devotion accessible to those who couldn't travel hundreds of miles, a dedicated shrine was established right here, next to the sacred Velaspur forest, establishing this spot as a local spiritual sanctuary.

Significance of Idols & Architecture

🚶‍♂️ The Divine Posture (Arms Akimbo)

The main idols of Lord Vitthal and Mother Rukmini are beautifully carved out of smooth black stone. Lord Vitthal stands in his classic, iconic posture—arms akimbo (hands resting on his hips), standing upon a brick (veet). This pose symbolizes patience, waiting, and eternal love for his devotees, welcoming all pilgrims who walk the sacred path.

🏛️ Bhajan Mandapa & Architecture

The temple structure blends simple Deccan rural architecture with traditional temple designs. It features an open assembly hall (Bhajan Mandapa) where devotees gather on the floor to sing, reflecting the egalitarian nature of the Warkari path where everyone is considered equal before God.

⏱️ Timings & Entry Details

The temple follows traditional Vaishnava timings, with a peaceful environment ideal for meditation and listening to devotional songs.

🎉 Festivals & Traditions

  • Ashadi Ekadashi & Kartiki Ekadashi
    These are the two most massive festivals celebrated at this mandir. During these days, the entire Mailar and Velaspur area echoes with chants of "Vitthal Vitthal Jai Hari Vitthal!" Thousands of local villagers dress in white, hold saffron flags (patakas), play the tal (cymbals), and perform a mini-Dindi Yatra (procession).
  • Daily Abhanga Bhajans
    A unique living tradition of this temple is the singing of holy verses composed by ancient saints like Saint Tukaram, Saint Dnyaneshwar, and Saint Namdev. The local community actively participates in keeping these musical traditions alive every single evening.
Travel Guide

How to Reach & Connectivity

📍

From Gaimukh / Mailar

Since it is located right in the vicinity of the Velaspur forest and Mailar Mallanna temple, visitors can easily cover this temple on foot or via a short local auto ride after visiting the other two shrines.

🚌

By Road

Accessible via the Bidar-Udgir highway route. It serves as a perfect stop for tourists planning a full day-trip covering the Mailar, Gaimukh, and Vitthal Rukmini temple circuit (~16–18 km from Bidar city center).
