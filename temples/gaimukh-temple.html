Gaimukh Temple

A sacred natural spring shrine where holy water continuously flows from a cow-mouth carved stone.

Sacred Chronicle

History & Heritage

The Local Legend of Velaspur Jungles

According to local folklore passed down by tribal and rural communities over hundreds of years, this deep forest valley of Velaspur was a meditation ground for ancient hermits (Sadhus). The legend says that while Lord Mailar Mallanna was fighting the demons Malla and Mani in the nearby Khanapur area, his divine forces rested in these Velaspur jungles.

To quench the thirst of the divine warriors and the local wildlife, Lord Shiva blessed this specific spot with an underground water source. Later, devotees carved a stone Gaimukh (cow's face) at the mouth of the spring to channel the water properly. The water continuously bathes a small, natural stone Shiva Lingam placed right beneath it.

Nature and Spiritual Heritage

🌳 Forest Valley Treks

Unlike regular city temples, this shrine maintains a raw, ancient connection with nature. Devotees often walk through the lush green pathways of the Velaspur forest valley to reach this tranquil, peaceful spot.

💧 Natural Abhishekam

The setup is completely natural—the water flows on its own from the rocky terrain, passes through the Gaimukh cow-mouth structure, and falls perfectly on the deity, performing a continuous, timeless Abhishekam.

🛡️ Historical Significance

For over a century, pastoral communities (like the Kurubas and local farmers) who herd cattle in the Velaspur forests have protected this shrine. They believe the water is blessed by the forest deities and keeps their livestock healthy.

⏱️ Timings & Entry Details

Because this temple is located inside a forested area, it is highly advised to visit during daylight hours for safety and the best natural view.

🎉 Festivals & Traditions

  • Maha Shivratri & Shravan Mondays
    During Shivratri and the holy month of Shravan, local villagers from Khanapur, Mailar, and Velaspur trek into the forest to offer milk, honey, and forest flowers to Lord Shiva.
  • The Holy Bath Ritual
    Devotees sit directly under the flowing Gaimukh stream to take a refreshing bath. The water contains forest medicinal properties (herbs from the roots of the jungle trees) that cure skin fatigue and bless the mind.
  • Vana Bhojan (Forest Feast)
    On special auspicious days, families come here to cook food over firewood in the open forest clearings near the temple as an offering to Nature and Lord Shiva.
Travel Guide

How to Reach & Connectivity

📍

From Mailar Temple

It is located just a short drive/trek away from the main Sri Mailar Mallanna Temple. You can ask local auto drivers at Mailar to take you toward the Velaspur forest route.

🚌

By Road from Bidar

It is roughly 16–18 km from Bidar city center. You take the Bidar-Udgir highway, turn toward Mailar/Khanapur, and head into the Velaspur valley road.

🥾

Trekking Route

The final stretch involves a scenic walk through the forest path. Wear comfortable shoes and stick to the marked trail.

💡 Pro-Tip for Visitors

Since the shrine is located deep inside the forest area, it is highly recommended to travel in groups and carry your own snacks and water bottles, as there are no commercial shops in the valley.
