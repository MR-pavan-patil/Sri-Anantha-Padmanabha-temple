About the Temple

A Divine Abode of
Lord Vishnu

Nestled deep within the verdant forests near Bidar, Karnataka, Sri Anantha Padmanabha Temple stands as a timeless testament to devotion and divine grace. This ancient temple, dedicated to Lord Vishnu in his eternal reclining form upon the cosmic serpent Anantha, offers a rare and profound spiritual experience where the sacred and the natural world converge in perfect harmony.

Surrounded by towering ancient trees, the gentle sounds of nature, and the fragrance of wildflowers, pilgrims and visitors alike find themselves enveloped in an atmosphere of unparalleled peace and serenity. The temple's sacred precincts have been a beacon of devotion for centuries, drawing seekers from far and wide.

Temple Highlights

Discover What Makes
This Temple Unique

From the divine presence of Lord Vishnu to the enchanting forest that cradles this sacred site, every aspect of Sri Anantha Padmanabha Temple is a wonder to behold.

01

Lord Vishnu

The presiding deity, Lord Anantha Padmanabha, rests in eternal repose upon the serpent Anantha Shesha. The divine idol radiates a serene, transcendent energy that inspires deep devotion and wonder.

02

Forest Location

Enveloped by a pristine, dense forest teeming with biodiversity, the temple's location is a natural sanctuary. Ancient trees stand as silent guardians, and the air is alive with the melodies of exotic birds.

03

Sacred Atmosphere

The convergence of spiritual chanting, flickering oil lamps, fragrant incense, and the surrounding forest creates an atmosphere of profound sacredness that touches every visitor's heart.

04

Pilgrimage Destination

Revered as a significant pilgrimage destination, devotees embark on the journey to seek blessings, fulfill vows, and experience the divine grace of Lord Padmanabha in this extraordinary forest setting.

Pilgrimage Timeline Route

1

Vishnu Mandir

Ancient shrine dedicated to Lord Vishnu, featuring exquisite stone carvings and serene forest clearings.

~3 KM
2

Mailar Mallanna Mandir

Hilltop temple dedicated to Lord Mallanna (a form of Shiva), offering panoramic views and vibrant festivals.

~7 KM
3

Shani Mahatma Mandir

Revered shrine dedicated to Lord Shani (Saturn), frequented by devotees seeking relief from planetary remedies.

~5 KM
4

Manik Prabhu Mandir

Spiritual center dedicated to saint Manik Prabhu, promoting interfaith harmony, samadhi shrine & gardens.

~12 KM
5

Vitthal Rukmini Mandir

Heart of Krishna Bhakti tradition on the trail, featuring soulful kirtans and daily devotional singing.

~8 KM
6

Gaimukh Temple

Sacred spring temple where holy water flows out of a cow-mouth shaped rock, believed to have healing properties.

~10 KM
