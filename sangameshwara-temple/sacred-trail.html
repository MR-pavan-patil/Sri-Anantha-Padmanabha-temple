The Sacred Trail

A spiritual guide to the prominent temples of the Bidar-Kosam region — connecting sacred spaces of local history, community faith, and nature.

Trail Overview

Embark on a
Spiritual Journey

The countryside of Bidar and Humnabad is home to a rich constellation of sacred shrines, each dedicated to a different deity and serving as an anchor of community faith. Together, these temples form a unique pilgrimage trail that has been traveled by locals for generations.

From the peaceful stone Shiva Lingam of Sangameshwara Temple in Kosam to the hilltop shrine of Mailar Mallanna, from the spiritual seat of Manik Prabhu in Maniknagar to the forest spring of Gaimukh, each site tells its own story of devotion, heritage, and peace.

Walking or driving through this countryside path connects you to the natural beauty of Karnataka's agricultural heartland, making the travel itself a meditative, calming experience.

Sacred Shrines

Temples Along
the Regional Trail

Explore the six prominent temples of this spiritual circuit, each offering a unique window into the heritage of the Deccan region.

Active Site
Temple 01

Shri Sangameshwara Temple

Nestled inside Kosam village, this sacred Shiva shrine represents the guardian deity of the surrounding farming communities. Known for its quiet countryside serenity, traditional harvest offerings, and uncommercialized atmosphere.

⭐ Central Shiva shrine of Kosam, agricultural protector and venue of Deepotsavam
View Details
~15 KM
Temple 02

Mailar Mallanna Mandir

Perched on a scenic hilltop in Khanapur village, this highly revered temple is dedicated to Lord Mallanna (Khandoba / Martanda Bhairava). Famous for its vibrant Bhandara tradition where turmeric powder is thrown.

⭐ Hilltop Shiva shrine managed by shepherd Kuruba priests, famous for Sunday market
View Details
~17 KM
Temple 03

Shani Mahatma Mandir

A powerful temple dedicated to Lord Shani (Saturn), located within the Mailar-Velaspur religious circuit. Devotees gather here in large numbers on Saturdays to perform oil rituals to ward off planetary obstacles.

⭐ Revered Shani shrine featuring a Navagraha complex and Saturday prayers
View Details
~18 KM
Temple 04

Manik Prabhu Mandir

Located in Maniknagar, Humnabad, this is the main seat of the Manik Prabhu Avadhuta Sampradaya. Dedicated to the 19th-century saint Shri Manik Prabhu Maharaj, who was revered as an incarnation of Lord Dattatreya.

⭐ Famous Dattatreya ashram promoting interfaith harmony and spiritual meditation
View Details
~16 KM
Temple 05

Vitthal Rukmini Mandir

Situated near the Velaspur forest borders, this temple dedicated to Lord Vitthal (Krishna) and Goddess Rukmini serves as the local center for the Warkari Bhakti tradition, holding daily abhanga chanting sessions.

⭐ Spiritual center for Vitthal Bhakti, daily abhangas, and local bhajan community
View Details
~16 KM
Temple 06

Gaimukh Temple

A unique rock-cut temple inside the Velaspur forest valley, where a natural perennial water spring flows out from a cow-mouth shaped stone. Pilgrims visit here to wash away sins and seek healing blessings.

⭐ Forest valley cave temple with sacred water spring of healing properties
View Details

One-Day Journey

A Complete Regional
Day on the Trail

Experience these diverse spiritual landmarks in a single day. Here is the recommended travel plan.

🌅
6:00 AM

Kosam Morning Calm

Arrive at Sangameshwara Temple in Kosam for early morning darshan. Watch the rural sunrise over sugarcane fields in total silence.

🧘
9:30 AM

Ashram Meditation

Drive to Maniknagar, Humnabad. Spend time in quiet meditation at the samadhi shrine and walk through the peaceful ashram gardens.

⛰️
1:00 PM

Hilltop Aarti

Reach Mailar Mallanna on the hilltop. Experience the unique Bhandara (turmeric) aarti and witness the traditional devotional chants.

🌇
4:30 PM

Forest Spring Closure

Complete the trail at Velaspur's Gaimukh temple. Wash your hands at the natural spring water and rest under banyan trees as the sun sets.
