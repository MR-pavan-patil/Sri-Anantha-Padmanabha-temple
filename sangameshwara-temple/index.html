About the Temple

A Divine Abode of
Lord Shiva

Nestled within the rustic and beautiful countryside of Kosam (Khasampur), near Bidar, Karnataka, Shri Sangameshwara Swamy Temple stands as a vital spiritual guardian for the surrounding agricultural villages. Worshipped in the sacred form of a stone Shiva Lingam, Lord Sangameshwara brings blessings of prosperity, fertile land, and deep spiritual peace to all who visit with a pure heart.

Surrounded by fertile farming fields and natural village tranquility, the temple provides a quiet, soul-soothing environment away from the city noise, making it the perfect destination for meditation, morning prayers, and experiencing authentic Deccani rural heritage.

Temple Highlights

Discover What Makes
This Temple Unique

From the divine presence of the stone Shiva Lingam to the peaceful agricultural fields that cradle it, every aspect of Shri Sangameshwara Swamy Temple is a wonder to behold.

01

Shiva Lingam

The presiding deity, Shri Sangameshwara Swamy, resides as a sacred stone Shiva Lingam in the main sanctum (Garbhagriha), radiating peace, strength, and divine protection.

02

Agricultural Setting

Located amidst the fertile farming villages of Bidar, the temple is surrounded by green crop fields, sugarcane, and old trees that create a natural sanctuary of calm.

03

Community Heritage

The structure and its traditions have been lovingly preserved by the local villagers of Kosam and Changler, maintaining its authentic, community-led religious roots.

04

Pilgrimage Destination

Revered by the surrounding region, devotees travel to Kosam to offer prayers before starting new ventures, seeking protection from drought and seeking prosperity.

Daily Spiritual Message

॥ ॐ नमः शिवाय ॥

"Om Namah Shivaya"

"Salutations to the auspicious Lord Shiva, the destroyer of darkness and source of ultimate peace."

॥ ॐ श्री गुरुदेव दत्त ॥

"Om Shri Gurudev Datta"

"Greetings to the divine trinity of Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva, embodied in Lord Dattatreya."

कर्पूरगौरं करुणावतारं संसारसारम् भुजगेन्द्रहारम्

"Karpuura-Gauram Karunna-Avataaram Samsaara-Saaram Bhujagendra-Haaram"

"Salutations to Shiva, pure as camphor, the compassion incarnate, who wears the serpent king."
