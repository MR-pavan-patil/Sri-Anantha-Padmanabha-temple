How To Reach

Finding Your Way to
Kosam Village

Shri Sangameshwara Swamy Temple is situated near Kosam village in the rural area of Bidar district, Karnataka. The location is easily accessible by road from neighboring towns.

🚗

By Road

Easily reached via local connecting roads branching off from the Humnabad-Bidar route. Hiring a private auto-rickshaw or riding a two-wheeler from Bidar or Humnabad is highly recommended.

Accessible via Humanabad-Bidar Route
🚂

By Train

Humanabad Railway Station and Bidar Railway Station are the nearest functional stations. From these stations, local auto-rickshaws or connecting road transport can be hired to reach Kosam.

Nearest Stations: Humanabad & Bidar
🚌

By Bus

State transport (KSRTC) and private bus operators run regular buses to Bidar and Humnabad bus stands from major cities like Hyderabad and Bangalore. Local connecting transport runs to nearby village lanes.

Deboard at Humnabad or Bidar Stand
✈️

By Air

Rajiv Gandhi International Airport in Hyderabad (HYD) is the closest international airport (~160 KM). From the airport, taxi services can be booked directly to Kosam. Bidar has limited domestic connectivity.

Nearest Major Airport: Hyderabad (HYD)
Visitor Information

Essential Pilgrimage Details

Understand local customs, harvest tradition offerings, dress rules, and available facilities to ensure a comfortable stay.

🪔 General Customs

  • Entry Fee: Entrance to the temple and surrounding pathways is completely free of charge.
  • Dress Code: Devotees are requested to dress modestly. Traditional Indian wear is recommended inside the stone sanctum.
  • Photography: Permitted in the outer crop fields and pathways, but strictly prohibited inside the main Garbhagriha.
  • Footwear: Shoes and sandals must be left at the free footwear stand before entering the inner courtyard.

🌾 Harvest Offerings (Nivedyam)

Following an age-old rural custom, local farmers bring fresh grains, sugarcane, or jowar to offer to Sangameshwara Swamy before starting their trade or consuming the season's yield.

  • Sowing Blessings: Farmers bring seed packets to place before the Shiva Lingam, seeking protection from drought.
  • Observance: Devotees are welcome to observe these agrarian rituals, which connect spirituality directly to the earth.
  • Participation: Visitors can buy fresh flowers or local grains from village stalls to offer during daily prayers.

Guidelines

Do's & Don'ts for Devotees

Help us preserve the quiet holiness and simple natural beauty of this rural village shrine.

✓ Recommended (Do's)

  • Dress modestly in simple, respectful clothing before entering the sanctum.
  • Maintain absolute silence inside the Mandapa and Garbhagriha areas.
  • Use the designated waste bins to discard any garbage or packing paper.
  • Respect the local farming fields; keep strictly to the village pathways.
  • Acknowledge the community-led efforts of the villagers maintaining the shrine.

✗ Prohibited (Don'ts)

  • Do not throw plastic, bottles, or food packets in the agricultural fields.
  • Do not attempt to feed village cattle or birds without permission.
  • Do not take photographs of the stone Shiva Lingam inside the sanctum.
  • Do not litter the pathways around the natural water channels.
  • Do not consume tobacco products or alcohol within the temple limits.
